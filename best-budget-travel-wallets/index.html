Quick answer

The best budget travel wallet is the BAGSMART RFID Travel Wallet for its secure zippered design and excellent organization.

The Lewis N. Clark is a close runner-up for those who prefer more pockets, while the Alpine Swiss is a popular slim choice for minimalists.

RFID-blocking travel wallets have gone from a niche gadget to a standard travel accessory. The logic is simple: identity thieves can use radio-frequency scanners to swipe your credit card numbers and passport data without ever touching you. A wallet with a built-in shield stops those scans cold. But not all budget-friendly options are created equal, some are bulky, some skimp on card slots, and others feel flimsy after a few trips. When shopping on a budget, focus on three things: security closure (zipper, snap, or band), number of card slots and compartments, and material durability. You want something that will hold your passport, boarding pass, several cards, and maybe a pen without turning into a brick in your pocket. The three wallets below have strong reputations among frequent travelers, and each suits a slightly different packing style.
